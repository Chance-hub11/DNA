Question 1 of 5
💸 The Discount Black Hole
When a rep asks for a 40% discount to close a deal, is the approval based on documented margin thresholds... or whoever they can find in Slack?
Why This Matters Every uncontrolled discount is money you'll never see. If your discount variance is 35% when best-in-class is 12%, you're leaking millions annually.
Slack Roulette
Reps DM whoever's online. Approval depends on who responds, their mood, and how much they like the rep. No thresholds. No logging.
Informal Guidelines
There's a "general sense" of what's acceptable but it's not written down. Different managers approve different things.
Partial Governance
Discount bands exist but aren't always followed. Big deals get reviewed, small deals slip through.
Mostly Governed
Clear discount tiers with approval levels. Most deals follow the rules. Exceptions logged and reviewed monthly.
Fully Governed
Automated approval workflows. Every discount logged with justification. Weekly variance reviews. Exception patterns eliminated.
Question 2 of 5
📊 The Forecast Fiction
If you asked 5 different reps what makes a deal "Stage 3" vs "Stage 4", would you get 5 different answers?
Why This Matters Your forecast accuracy is a direct result of your stage criteria clarity. Tribal definitions = 65% accuracy. Evidence-based criteria = 90%+. Every 10% error costs you in planning, hiring, and board credibility.
Vibes-Based Staging
Stage means whatever the rep feels like. "Stage 3 = I've had a good meeting." No criteria. 5 reps = 5 completely different answers.
Informal Criteria
There are some general stage descriptions, but they're vague. "Qualified opportunity" means different things to different people.
Documented But Ignored
Stage criteria exist in a doc somewhere. Some reps follow them. Others game the system. Managers don't consistently enforce.
Evidence-Required
Clear criteria with required evidence (emails, meeting notes). Managers review stage accuracy. Most reps aligned.
System-Enforced
CRM enforces stage criteria. Can't advance without required evidence. Automatic alerts on stale deals. Stage = objective fact, not opinion.
Question 3 of 5
🧠 The Genius Closer
Could your top rep explain exactly WHY they win deals in a way that a new hire could replicate... or do they "just know"?
Why This Matters If your win rate variance is 3x between top and bottom quartile, that's not a talent problem — it's a knowledge capture problem. Your "genius" isn't replicable because the logic isn't codified. You're one resignation away from losing it all.
Pure Magic
"Sarah just has the touch." Nobody can explain why she wins 3x more than others. No playbook. New hires shadow for 6+ months and still don't get it.
Tribal Wisdom
Top performers can give tips, but they're abstract. "Build rapport." "Understand the business." Nothing specific enough to replicate.
Partial Playbook
Some best practices documented. MEDDIC or similar exists on paper. Top performers do more, but it's not captured.
Documented Method
Clear sales methodology with specific actions for each stage. Win/loss analysis feeds the playbook. New hires effective in 60-90 days.
Institutional Knowledge
Complete playbook with evidence cards, objection handling, competitive positioning. Continuously updated. Win rate variance <1.5x across reps.
Question 4 of 5
🗺️ The Territory Knife Fight
When you last rebalanced territories, was it based on data-driven TAM analysis... or whoever screamed loudest?
Why This Matters Your territory productivity variance is probably 4x or more. That's not market variation — that's design failure. You're under-covering millions in TAM and over-covering others. And you're losing good reps to bad territories.
Political Warfare
Territories assigned based on tenure, relationships, and who complains most. No data. The loudest voice wins. Good reps quit over bad territories.
Gut + Politics
Leadership makes decisions based on "feel" with some account data. Politics still play a major role. Rebalancing avoided because it causes fights.
Data-Informed
Account data is used but not TAM-based. Some analysis, but final decisions still involve negotiation. Rebalancing happens annually (too slow).
Data-Driven
TAM-based territory design. Clear criteria for assignment. Quarterly reviews. Most decisions are objective. Some political override for edge cases.
Algorithmic Optimization
System-optimized territories based on TAM, rep capacity, and historical conversion. Continuous rebalancing. Variance <2x across territories. Zero politics.
Question 5 of 5
💵 The Commission Confusion
When a rep disputes their commission or asks for a special SPIF, is the resolution based on documented rules... or who has the better relationship with their manager?
Why This Matters Your exception rate is probably 20%+ (industry best is 8%). Every exception that's not logged is a lawsuit waiting to happen. And inconsistent treatment breeds resentment that kills culture and retention.
Relationship-Based
Disputes resolved by whoever the rep knows. Different reps get different treatment. Shadow SPIFs happen all the time. Nothing logged.
Manager Discretion
Managers handle disputes with broad discretion. Some consistency within teams, but wide variance across managers.
Guidelines Exist
Written comp plan with exception guidelines. Disputes go to comp team. Some logging. But exceptions still common and not always consistent.
Governed Process
Clear escalation path. All exceptions logged with justification. Monthly review of exception patterns. Most decisions feel fair.
Fully Transparent
System-calculated commissions with complete audit trail. Disputes resolved by documented criteria. Every decision explainable and defensible.
